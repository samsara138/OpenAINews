The Canadian federal government is going to invest more than $500 million into the recovery from flooding in British Columbia.

Google News

The Federal Government is providing British Columbia with more than $500 million to help cover the costs of the devastating floods in 2021. This money is being provided through the Disaster Financial Assistance Arrangements (DFAA) program, bringing the total amount given to British Columbia to over $1 billion. The flooding resulted in five deaths and displaced thousands of people, as well as destroying farmlands, damaging homes, infrastructure, and livestock. The Insurance Bureau of Canada estimates that the cost of insured flood damage is at least $450 million. Provinces and territories have up to five years to submit their final claim for DFAA assistance.
